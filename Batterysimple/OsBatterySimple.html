Memory

N-Back Paradigm

The N-back task has been widely used to assess Working Memory (WM) functions. "N" is a variable that refers to a level at which participants are asked to decide whether the presented stimuli are the same as "n" previous stimuli. The most common "n" levels are n=1, n=2, n=3, and n=0 (control condition).

In a typical N-2 Back task, participants are presented with a series of stimuli one-by-one (strings, images, or sounds). Their task is to decide whether the presented stimulus is the same as the one presented two times before (N-2). If it is, a response would be a keypress. Otherwise, they should not respond. The correct response would be a key press if the stimulus is the same, and the false alarm would be a key press to different stimuli (Figure 2).

The task requires sustained performance rather than individual responses throughout the trial and this ensures that working memory is “on-line” during the task.
